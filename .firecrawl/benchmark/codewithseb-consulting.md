FAQ

## Common Questions

01

### How is this different from hiring a typical consultant?

Most consultants give advice from the sidelines and leave. I work inside your codebase — I open PRs, review your team's code, pair on hard bugs, and sit in your standups if that's useful. Advice is cheap when nobody has to ship it. I do both.

02

### How do you work alongside existing engineers?

As a peer, not as an overseer. I listen first, read the code, understand the history and constraints, and then propose changes collaboratively. The goal is to raise the bar on quality and unblock hard problems — not to take credit or override your team.

03

### Do you also write code, or just review it?

Both. I'm a hands-on senior developer — reviewing code is a core part of the job, but I also ship production features, prototype AI integrations, and pair on the problems your team has been stuck on. If you just need a reviewer, that's fine too.

04

### Can you help with technical due diligence?

Yes. I regularly help with the technical side of investor conversations: honest assessments of codebases, architecture risk reports, and AI integration readiness. Investors want to know the tech is solid — I help you prove it (or find out it isn't, before they do).

05

### How quickly can you ramp up?

Fast. AI-assisted codebase analysis means I understand your architecture in hours, not weeks. I typically spend 2-3 days in deep-dive mode, and by the end of the first week I'm opening meaningful PRs. By week 3, I'm fully productive in your codebase.
